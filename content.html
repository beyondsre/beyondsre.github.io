Understanding 9 Pillars of SRE

Shayan Saha
Site Reliability Engineering Enthusiast
May 29, 2026

As SREs, one of the most common interview questions we hear is
What are the 9 pillars of Site Reliability Engineering?
Most of us know the concept but explaining them in simple language is where things get tricky. So here’s one good and simple explanation of the 9 pillars of SRE using everyday analogies

1️⃣ Culture | Playing Nicely Together 🤝

Imagine your friend accidentally bumps the table and knocks over a tower.
Nobody blames them.
Instead, everyone says: "How can we build this better so it won’t fall next time?"
That’s SRE culture: Focus on learning and improving systems, not blaming people.


2️⃣ Work Sharing | Everyone Helps Clean Up 🧹

You don’t make one person clean up all the Lego blocks while everyone else keeps playing.
Everyone helps with both: ✅ Building cool things ✅ Keeping the room tidy
In SRE, developers and operations share responsibility together.


3️⃣ Toil Reduction | The Cleanup Robot 🤖

Picking up 1,000 Lego pieces by hand every day is boring.
So you build a robot vacuum to do it automatically.
Now you have more time for fun and creativity.
That’s automation in SRE: Eliminate repetitive manual work.


4️⃣ SLOs & Error Budgets | The “Oops” Allowance 🚀

You and your friends agree:
"We can accidentally break our spaceship 3 times today while trying cool tricks."
But after the 4th time? No more tricks. Now it’s time to make the spaceship stronger.
That’s how SRE balances: ⚡ Innovation 🛡️ Reliability


5️⃣ Measurement | The Growth Chart 📏

How do you know which toy car is fastest?
You use a stopwatch.
How do you know which tower is tallest?
You use a ruler.
In SRE: If you can’t measure it, you can’t improve it.


6️⃣ Anti-Fragility | The Bouncy Castle 🏰

Instead of building something fragile like glass…
You build a bouncy castle that survives falls.
You even practice failures on purpose so the system becomes stronger.
That’s resilience engineering.


7️⃣ Deployments | The Tiny Bite Rule 🍽️

When trying a new vegetable, you don’t eat the whole plate at once.
You take one tiny bite first.
If it’s good → continue. If not → stop immediately.
That’s exactly how safe deployments work: Small releases reduce risk.


8️⃣ Performance Management | Enough Swings for Everyone 🎠

If 10 kids arrive but there’s only 1 swing…
Everyone waits and gets frustrated.
Good systems ensure there are always enough resources so users don’t experience delays.


9️⃣ Incident Management | Fixing Boo-Boos 🩹

When a child falls:
First → treat the injury. Then → remove the rock that caused the fall.
SRE works the same way:
Restore service quickly
Fix the root cause permanently


SRE is not just about servers, alerts, and uptime. It’s about building systems and teams that learn, adapt, and improve continuously.
